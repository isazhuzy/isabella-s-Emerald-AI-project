Intake call — Merrymeeting Group / Senior Accountant search
Participants: Recruiter (Emerald), Hiring Manager (Controller, Merrymeeting Group)

Recruiter: Thanks for the time. Let's scope the Senior Accountant role. Tell me about Merrymeeting and the team.

HM: Sure. Merrymeeting Group is a holding company — we've built and acquired businesses across a bunch of industries, pretty entrepreneurial. The finance team supports several operating entities. This person gets a lot of visibility to leadership. Culture is collaborative, we think long-term, we promote from within.

Recruiter: Where is the role based?

HM: Independence, Ohio. On-site, but we'll look at people within about a 30-mile radius. Cleveland metro basically.

Recruiter: What will they actually be doing day to day?

HM: Managing day-to-day accounting for assigned business units. Monthly financial statements and reporting packages, the analysis around them. General ledger maintenance, account reconciliations. They own month-end and year-end close support. Keeping the books accurate and on time. Maintaining our accounting policies and internal controls. Partnering with leadership to improve processes. Helping with the annual audit and coordinating with external auditors. Plus ad hoc financial analysis and special projects.

Recruiter: What's the must-have background?

HM: Bachelor's in Accounting. Four to eight years of progressive accounting experience. Strong on general ledger and financial reporting. They have to have done month-end close and reconciliations. Excel power user — pivot tables, lookups — and comfortable in an ERP. Detail-oriented, organized, can juggle priorities and hit deadlines. Good written and verbal communication.

Recruiter: CPA required?

HM: Nice to have, not required. We're not looking for pure audit or assurance people though — we want operational/corporate accounting, not someone straight out of a Big Four audit track, and definitely not interns.

Recruiter: Comp and what makes this attractive?

HM: Competitive package, semi-annual bonus potential. Full medical, dental, vision, 401(k). The real selling points: high visibility within the org, collaborative team-oriented culture, and genuine room to grow and advance within the accounting function. Long-term opportunity.

Recruiter: Great, that's plenty to build the search.
